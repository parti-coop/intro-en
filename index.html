How we work

The practice came first, not the tools

For over a decade Parti has convened up to twenty offline deliberations, living labs, and civic workshops every month — conservatively, 1,000+ field events. Every platform we built grew out of what we first learned in real rooms with real citizens.

Trust · rules · stewardship

A civic platform is not just code. It works when trust, procedural rules, and a responsible operating body are built together — a combination we have been learning to get right for ten years, alongside the institutions that use DemosX.

Citizen ownership, by structure

Parti is a cooperative: the square is owned by the people who use and run it. Governments adopt our platforms, but the ownership stays with citizens — that independence has held across changes of administration.

Knowledge as a public good

We publish our deliberation playbooks, formats, and operating guides — including deliberative programs such as Galaxy Voting, Star-to-Star Dialogue, and Policy Battles — so others can adapt them. toolkit.parti.coop

Context

Twenty years of digital democracy in Korea

How does a society carry the energy of its plazas into everyday institutions? Korea has lived that question in a compressed form — repeated crises, massive candlelight plazas, and each time, an attempt to turn that energy into durable civic infrastructure. Parti grew up inside this history, as one thread among many. For the full story, read our CEO's essay: A Genealogy of Korean Digital Democracy.

2004 – 2008

Agora — the first mass online public square

Daum's "Agora" forum became the largest online public square of 2000s Korea, where millions debated national issues daily. It showed that ordinary citizens would deliberate at scale — and it is where Parti's story begins: our CEO worked on Agora before leaving to build squares that citizens themselves could own.

2008

Candlelight protests — the online square moves to the street

The protests against US beef imports were organized substantially through online forums, an early landmark of internet-mediated civic action.

2008 protests Candlelight rallies in Korea
2015

Parti is founded

Developers and activists from the portal-era squares founded Parti Demos, on a conviction the portals could not deliver: the digital civic square should belong to citizens. In 2018 it was incorporated as a social cooperative.

parti.coop
2016 – 2017

The candlelight plazas and a peaceful transfer of power

Millions gathered weekly through a winter of candlelight demonstrations, leading to a presidential impeachment entirely within constitutional process. The question the plazas left behind — how to make participation everyday rather than exceptional — has shaped Korean civic tech since.

2016–2017 protests
2017 –

Participation platforms enter government

The post-impeachment government opened "Gwanghwamun 1st Avenue" for citizen proposals, and Seoul launched "Democracy Seoul." Parti helped build and operate these platforms — for us, the beginning of the civic collaboration model that later became DemosX.

demosx.org
2019

Facing the Ocean — East Asian civic tech joins hands

Civic hackers from Korea, Japan, and Taiwan began meeting and hacking together as Facing the Ocean — a network Parti helped propose and organize with g0v and Code for Japan, starting in Okinawa 2019.

Code for Japan Taiwan Insight essay g0v Summit review
2020

COVID-19 — civic hackers and open data in 3 days

When mask shortages hit, Parti proposed a joint civic-tech response for public data. The government opened pharmacy inventory data; within 3 days citizen developers shipped the first apps — 200+ volunteers, 50+ free apps. The collaboration grew into Code for Korea.

codefor.kr
2022 – 2024

Deliberation becomes infrastructure

DemosX spread across central ministries, metropolitan government, education offices, and the presidential National Unity Commission — the same civic collaboration model, adopted as continuing infrastructure rather than one-off projects.

Dec 2024

The martial law crisis — and citizens, again

A sudden martial law declaration was overturned within hours by the National Assembly, surrounded and protected by citizens; impeachment and a peaceful election followed. Korea's democracy held — and the crisis renewed the case that democratic capacity must live in everyday infrastructure, not only in emergencies.

2024 martial law crisis
2025 – 2026

AI meets deliberation

DemosX now uses AI to analyze tens of thousands of citizen opinions so that citizens themselves surface the agenda (National Unity Commission). Parti designs AI-supported one-on-one deep dialogue and public forums with the Ministry of Unification, and our CEO serves on the AI Democracy Division of the presidential National AI Strategy Council.
